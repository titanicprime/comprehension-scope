# Comprehension Scope Protocol

A minimal, universal protocol for making sense of claims, sources, and situations in high-entropy environments.

This is not a truth oracle.  
It is a **scaffold** for your own judgment.

Use it on anything:

- news  
- social media  
- expert claims  
- institutional statements  
- AI outputs  
- conversations  

---

## 1. What is being claimed?

**Goal:** Compress the claim into a single clear sentence.

- What is the core assertion?  
- Can you say it in your own words?  
- Can you remove emotional language and keep the meaning?

If it cannot be stated clearly, it cannot be evaluated clearly.

---

## 2. What would make this false?

**Goal:** Identify the falsification condition.

- What observation or fact would contradict this claim?  
- Is there any possible world in which this is wrong?  
- Is the claim framed so vaguely that it can never be wrong?

If nothing could make it false, it is not a claim—it is persuasion or belief.

---

## 3. What incentives shape this?

**Goal:** Map incentives, not motives.

- Who is making or amplifying this claim?  
- What do they gain if you believe it?  
- What do they lose if you ignore it?  
- What are their financial, social, political, or reputational payoffs?

You do not need to know their inner motives.  
You only need to see their incentive structure.

---

## 4. What is the simplest alternative explanation?

**Goal:** Avoid overfitting to dramatic or conspiratorial stories.

- Is there a more ordinary explanation that fits the facts?  
- Are you assuming coordination where simple error or noise would suffice?  
- Are you drawn to this explanation because it feels exciting, confirming, or enraging?

Prefer explanations that require fewer assumptions, unless strong evidence demands otherwise.

---

## 5. What action does this push me toward?

**Goal:** Separate comprehension from manipulation.

- If you fully accepted this claim, what would you be inclined to do?  
- Who benefits from you taking that action?  
- Do you actually want to take that action, given your own goals and values?

Every claim is also an attempt to shape behavior.  
See the intended action before you decide your own.

---

## How to use this in practice

You do not need to run the full protocol every time.

- In fast contexts (social media, headlines):  
  - Ask: **“What is being claimed?”** and **“What action does this push me toward?”**

- In slow contexts (long articles, big decisions):  
  - Walk through all five questions deliberately.  
  - Write your answers down if the stakes are high.

Over time, these questions become habits.  
The goal is not to distrust everything.  
The goal is to **see more clearly** before you commit attention, belief, or action.

---

## What this is not

- It is not a replacement for expertise.  
- It is not a guarantee of truth.  
- It is not a political project.  
- It is not a filter bubble or a scoring system.

It is a **cognitive safety rail**:  
small, simple, and always available.
